\bigskip

This codebook documents every column that appears in the pipeline output files
(\texttt{*.csv} / \texttt{*.json}).

\textbf{Coding principle.}
All indicators code \textit{de facto} (actual) power, not de jure (formal) arrangements. (We might want to discuss about this)

\medskip
\textbf{Default run settings.}
The pipeline is run with \texttt{--verify self\_consistency --n-samples 2},
which makes 3 total LLM calls per indicator (1 initial + 2 additional samples).
This produces per-indicator columns \texttt{\_SC1}, \texttt{\_SC2}, \texttt{\_SC3},
plus \texttt{\_prediction} (majority vote), \texttt{\_agreement}, and \\ \texttt{\_uncertainty}.
If \texttt{--n-samples 1} is used, only \texttt{\_SC1} and \texttt{\_SC2} appear.

\section{Indicator Output Columns}
% ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Each indicator produces the following columns (replace \texttt{\{ind\}} with
the indicator name, \\ e.g.\ \texttt{sovereign\_prediction}).

\ra{1.3}
\begin{longtable}{P{4.5cm} P{2.3cm} P{7.7cm}}
  \toprule
  \thead{Column} & \thead{Type} & \thead{Meaning} \\
  \midrule
  \endhead
  \bottomrule
  \endfoot
  \texttt{\{ind\}\_prediction}  & int         & LLM prediction label \\
  \texttt{\{ind\}\_reasoning}   & string      & Step-by-step reasoning text (If any) \\
  \texttt{\{ind\}\_confidence}  & int (1–100) & LLM self-reported confidence score \\
  \texttt{\{ind\}\_SC1}         & int / null  & Prediction from sample 1 \\
  \texttt{\{ind\}\_SC2}         & int / null  & Prediction from sample 2 \\
  \texttt{\{ind\}\_SC3}         & int / null  & Prediction from sample 3 (with \texttt{--n-samples 2}) \\
  \texttt{\{ind\}\_prediction}    & int / null  & Majority-vote prediction across all samples; null if no consensus \\
  \texttt{\{ind\}\_agreement}   & float (0–1) & Fraction of samples agreeing with the majority label \\
  \texttt{\{ind\}\_uncertainty} & string      & \texttt{none} = all agree; \texttt{low} = partial; \texttt{high} = all differ \\
\end{longtable}

\subsection*{Indicator Label Definitions}

\ra{1.3}
\begin{longtable}{P{3.2cm} P{2.5cm} P{8.8cm}}
  \toprule
  \thead{Indicator} & \thead{Values} & \thead{Label Meanings} \\
  \midrule
  \endhead
  \bottomrule
  \endfoot
  \texttt{constitution}   & 0, 1, 2    &
    0 = no written law;\newline
    1 = code of law (governs subjects only);\newline
    2 = full constitution (written law + governance rules + limits on authority) \\[4pt]
  \texttt{sovereign}      & 0, 1       &
    0 = colony, vassal, tributary, or protectorate;\newline
    1 = sovereign domestic governance \\[4pt]
  \texttt{federalism}     & 0, 1       &
    0 = unitary state;\newline
    1 = sovereignty divided between central and local units
          (confederations, leagues, composite monarchies) \\[4pt]
  \texttt{collegiality}   & 0, 1       &
    0 = single actor dominates decision-making;\newline
    1 = decisions genuinely shared by a constituted body \\[4pt]
  \texttt{petition}       & 0, 1       &
    0 = petitioning is rare, ineffective, or unrecorded;\newline
    1 = petitions are a regular and institutionalized feature of political life \\[4pt]
  \texttt{assembly}       & 0, 1, 2, 3 &
    0 = none;\newline
    1 = small advisory council (institutionalized);\newline
    2 = legislature with policymaking or selection role;\newline
    3 = popular assembly (most citizens or chosen by lot) \\[4pt]
  \texttt{symbolism}      & 0, 1, 2, 3 &
    \textit{Non-monotonic — higher code does not mean more power.}\newline
    0 = plain (ruler little distinguished from others);\newline
    1 = decorated (impressive but officeholder understood as mortal);\newline
    2 = deified (holder regarded as divine or quasi-divine);\newline
    3 = ceremonial (trappings so extensive they constrain power) \\[4pt]
  \texttt{entry}          & 0–10, 99   &
    How the executive came to power:\newline
    0 = force (coup, rebellion, conquest);\newline
    1 = foreign power appointment;\newline
    2 = ruling party appointment (one-party system);\newline
    3 = royal council appointment;\newline
    4 = hereditary succession;\newline
    5 = military appointment;\newline
    6 = legislature appointment;\newline
    7 = head of state appointment;\newline
    8 = head of government appointment;\newline
    9 = direct popular election;\newline
    10 = other (e.g., clerical bodies such as College of Cardinals);\newline
    99 = unknown \\[4pt]
  \texttt{exit}           & 0–14, 99   &
    Circumstances of departure:\newline
    0 = voluntary retirement or abdication (not due to ill health);\newline
    1 = other regular exit (term limits, electoral defeat);\newline
    2 = regular transition to another office;\newline
    3 = died on campaign in civil war (disease or accident);\newline
    4 = died on campaign in foreign war (disease or accident);\newline
    5 = died of natural causes;\newline
    6 = retired due to ill health;\newline
    7 = suicide;\newline
    8 = deposed by domestic actors;\newline
    9 = assassinated or forced suicide;\newline
    10 = killed in battle in civil war;\newline
    11 = killed in battle in foreign war;\newline
    12 = irregular transition to another office;\newline
    13 = deposed by foreign state;\newline
    14 = still in office at end of observation period;\newline
    99 = unknown \\
\end{longtable}

\section{Checks Sub-Indicator Columns}
% ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The \texttt{checks} indicator is split into nine binary sub-indicators coding
whether that actor \textit{actually} checks the executive
(0 = does not check; 1 = does check).
Each sub-indicator generates the same column set as any other indicator
(\texttt{\_prediction}, \texttt{\_reasoning}, \texttt{\_confidence},
\texttt{\_SC1}, \texttt{\_SC2}, \texttt{\_SC3},
\texttt{\_verified}, \texttt{\_agreement}, \texttt{\_uncertainty}).

\ra{1.3}
\begin{longtable}{P{5cm} P{9.5cm}}
  \toprule
  \thead{Sub-indicator prefix} & \thead{Actor} \\
  \midrule
  \endhead
  \bottomrule
  \endfoot
  \texttt{checks\_local}        & Local or constituent territorial/kin-based units \\
  \texttt{checks\_military}     & Armed forces or a warrior estate \\
  \texttt{checks\_clergy}       & Religious authorities \\
  \texttt{checks\_aristocracy}  & Hereditary or titled upper stratum \\
  \texttt{checks\_bourgeoisie}  & Urban commercial or capital-holding class \\
  \texttt{checks\_bureaucracy}  & Professional administrative apparatus \\
  \texttt{checks\_judiciary}    & Independent adjudicative bodies \\
  \texttt{checks\_assembly}     & Deliberative representative or popular body \\
  \texttt{checks\_council}      & Institutionalized elite advisory council \\
\end{longtable}

\section{Elections Column (Downstream)}
% ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Run separately after the main pipeline (applies for both standard and batch pipelines). Rows where \texttt{assembly\_prediction}~$\neq$~2 receive a
pass-through label of 0 with no API call (hard coded).

\ra{1.3}
\begin{longtable}{P{4.8cm} P{2.3cm} P{7.4cm}}
  \toprule
  \thead{Column} & \thead{Type} & \thead{Meaning} \\
  \midrule
  \endhead
  \bottomrule
  \endfoot
  \texttt{elections\_prediction} & int         &
    0 = not elected (or pass-through when \texttt{assembly}~$\neq$~2);\newline
    1 = elected, no organised factions;\newline
    2 = competitive elections (organised factions or parties) \\
  \texttt{elections\_SC1}        & int / null    & Prediction from sample 1 \\
  \texttt{elections\_SC2}        & int / null    & Prediction from sample 2 \\
  \texttt{elections\_SC3}        & int / null    & Prediction from sample 3 (with \texttt{--n-samples 2}) \\
  \texttt{elections\_reasoning}  & string / null & Reasoning text; null for pass-through rows \\
  \texttt{elections\_confidence} & int / null    & Confidence 1–100; null for pass-through rows \\
  \texttt{elections\_prediction}   & int / null    & Majority-vote prediction; null if no consensus \\
  \texttt{elections\_agreement}  & float (0-1)   & Fraction of samples agreeing with the majority label \\
  \texttt{elections\_uncertainty}& string        & \texttt{none} / \texttt{low} / \texttt{high} \\
\end{longtable}
